I am a Postdoctoral fellow at the Data Sciences and Computing, Sandia National Laboratory. I'm actively engaged in cutting-edge research, developing innovative methodologies, and contributing to the broader scientific community. My expertise lies in the nexus of high-performance computing, numerical modeling, and machine learning, focusing on solving problems in mechanics and materials.

Throughout my career, my research has mainly centered around computational models for fluid and solid mechanics, developing advanced deep-learning models to predict material properties, and leveraging Bayesian optimization for real-time decision-making in engineering applications.
I have worked on problems such as multiscale finite element methods, physics-informed neural networks, uncertainty quantification, and active learning. 

In addition, I am interested in researching how to make ML models more reliable and interpretable. With this aim, I have been working on finding metrics that can indicate where and why these models might fail and also levering knowledge from explainable AI and adversarial AI.
